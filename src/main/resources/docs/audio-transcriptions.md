# 📝 Audio Transcriptions

> Turn radio talk into text automatically!

Sometimes radio audio can be static-filled, fast-paced, or hard to understand. SDRTrunk Kennebec features an incredible built-in transcription engine that listens to the audio and types out what is being said in real-time.

## 🎙️ How It Works

Think of this like turning on "Closed Captions" for your radio scanner. 

When a police officer or dispatcher speaks:
1. Kennebec's built-in AI quickly listens to the MP3 recording.
2. It translates the spoken words into text.
3. The text is attached to the call log, so you can read what was said!

> [!NOTE]
> The transcription happens securely right on your computer. Your audio is not sent to random cloud servers, which helps protect the privacy of the radio calls.

## 🚀 Why Use Transcriptions?

* **🔍 Easy Searching:** Trying to find a specific call? You can search your logs for words like "Fire" or "Main Street" instead of listening to hours of audio.
* **👂 Accessibility:** Great for users who are hard of hearing or scanning in a noisy room.
* **📱 Streaming Text:** When sending calls to platforms like Rdio Scanner, the text is uploaded alongside the audio!

## ⚙️ How to Turn it On

1. Open your **User Preferences**.
2. Navigate to the **AI & Processing** tab.
3. Check the box for **Enable Audio Transcriptions**.

> [!WARNING]
> Transcribing audio requires a bit of extra computer power. If you are running Kennebec on a very old or slow laptop, you might want to leave this feature off so things run smoothly.
